## Priority lesson-pack structure

Each lesson pack should include:

- one-sentence teaching goal
- recommended version of Avida-ED
- estimated class time
- student-facing instructions
- teacher notes and common pitfalls
- expected deliverables

## Legacy lesson-pack set to migrate

### Understanding the introduction of genetic variation by random mutation

- Audience: first-time Avida-ED users
- Focus: mutations as a source of genetic variability and what it means for mutation to occur randomly
- Deliverable: short explanation supported by observations about genomic change
- HTML-first page: [Exercise 1: Random Mutation and Genetic Variation](./exercise-1-random-mutation.md)

### From genotype to phenotype

- Audience: courses introducing the relationship between genotype, phenotype, and environment
- Focus: how genomic changes lead to changes in observable functional traits
- Deliverable: explanation linking genome changes to phenotypic outcomes

### Exploring the effects of mutation rate on individuals

- Audience: classes analyzing the biological effects of increased mutation rate
- Focus: student testing of mutation-rate effects on individual organisms
- Deliverable: comparison of outcomes plus evidence-based explanation

### Exploring the effects of mutation rate on populations

- Audience: classes ready to work with population-level measures
- Focus: how mutation rate can affect average fitness and population size
- Deliverable: comparison table or graph plus explanation

### Artificial selection: evolution in practice

- Audience: introductory evolution classes
- Focus: using artificial selection as a practical entry point into evolutionary change
- Deliverable: student plan and explanation of how trait-focused selection changes outcomes

### Exploring selection and fitness

- Audience: classes connecting variation, selection, and fitness
- Focus: controlled experiments leading to hypotheses about how resistance evolves
- Deliverable: data-backed explanation of the relationship between selection and fitness

### Experimental evolution project with evolving digital organisms

- Audience: longer inquiry-based units
- Focus: controlled comparisons, claims, evidence, and reasoning
- Deliverable: lab-style report or presentation
