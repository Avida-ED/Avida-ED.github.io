## Avida-ED lab book

The legacy site's most complete entry point was the **Avida-ED lab book**, a sequence of exercises that introduced both the software mechanics and the core evolutionary ideas instructors usually want students to investigate first.

The lab book collection includes:

- 2019 lab book overview PDF
- introduction/background section
- four structured exercises
- an independent research project
- a quick-start user manual
- linked explanatory videos for several exercises

Key items from the legacy site:

- Lab Book 2019 version
- Introduction
- Exercise 1: introduction of genetic variation by random mutation
- Exercise 2: random mutation and selection
- Exercise 3: fitness and population change under selection
- Exercise 4: population change without selection
- Independent research project
- Quick Start User Manual

These should be migrated over time into HTML-first pages, but they remain useful as a content backbone for the new site.

## Model lessons migrated from the legacy site

The old curriculum page highlighted several peer-reviewed model lessons:

- Understanding the introduction of genetic variation by random mutation
- From genotype to phenotype
- Exploring the effects of mutation rate on individuals
- Exploring the effects of mutation rate on populations
- Artificial selection: evolution in practice
- Exploring selection and fitness
- Experimental evolution project with evolving digital organisms

Most of these lessons had:

- a student handout
- instructor support material
- notes on classroom implementation

The legacy page also noted that some materials were designed for older Avida-ED interfaces. That distinction matters now: the new site should keep the concepts and activity structure while updating screenshots and version guidance for Avida-ED 4 as the default.

## What belongs here

This section is for materials that support teaching and learning directly:

- lesson plans and structured activities
- student handouts and reporting prompts
- data-analysis guidance
- references to videos and walkthroughs that support the activity

## Suggested path

1. Use [Choose a Version](../get-started/choose-version.md) to confirm whether your class should start with Avida-ED 4 or legacy Avida-ED 3.
2. Pick an activity type from [Classroom Activities](./classroom-activities.md).
3. Use [Teacher Quick Start](../teachers/quick-start.md) to plan what students will submit.
4. Keep [Support](../support/index.md) available during class for troubleshooting.
