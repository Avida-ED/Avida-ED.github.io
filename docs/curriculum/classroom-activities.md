---
title: Classroom Activities
---

Use these patterns when deciding how Avida-ED will fit into your class schedule.

## Single-period lab

Best for a first exposure or a focused concept check.

- Goal: run one baseline condition and one comparison condition
- Student output: short explanation, table of results, or exit ticket
- Good fit for: variation, mutation, and adaptation introductions

## Two- to three-class sequence

Best for deeper comparison and discussion.

- Goal: compare multiple conditions across groups
- Student output: shared data table, short graph, group explanation
- Good fit for: selection, environmental change, and interpreting evidence

## Multi-week inquiry project

Best for student-designed investigations.

- Goal: students pose and test their own question using controlled comparisons
- Student output: proposal, data set, report or presentation
- Good fit for: experimental design, evidence-based explanation, scientific reasoning
