This page collects the publication PDFs currently bundled with the curriculum
materials and points instructors to the main reasons they might use them.

## Curriculum design and teaching use

- [An Avida-ED digital evolution curriculum for undergraduate biology](/curriculum/publications/Smithetal2016.pdf)

Use this paper when you want a concise description of how the Avida-ED
curriculum was structured for undergraduate biology teaching.

## Student learning and assessment

- [Exploring the relationship between experiences with digital evolution and students' scientific understanding and acceptance of evolution](/curriculum/publications/ABT_Exploring_Relationship__Understanding_Acceptance_Evo.pdf)
- [Evolution in Action in the Classroom: Engaging Students in Science Practices to Investigate and Explain Evolution by Natural Selection](/curriculum/publications/ABT_Online_Feb_2018.pdf)

Use these when you need evidence about how students respond to Avida-ED-based
instruction, especially around scientific reasoning, evolution learning, and
engagement with evidence.

## How to use this page

- Read these alongside [Quick Start](./quick-start.md) when planning a first
  classroom use.
- Read them alongside [Digital Evolution](/about/digital-evolution) when you
  need the broader conceptual background for a course, report, or proposal.
