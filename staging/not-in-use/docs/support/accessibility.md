Avida-ED aims to support accessible classroom use for all learners.

## Accessibility practices on this site

- clear headings and predictable navigation
- descriptive link text
- visible link styling that does not rely on color alone
- alt text for meaningful images
- keyboard-usable navigation
- captions and transcripts when video support material is provided

## Current practical guidance

For the redesigned site, the most reliable accessibility supports are:

- HTML-first guidance for student and instructor workflows
- transcript pages for video material as they are migrated
- support pages that explain tasks in text instead of relying on embedded media

If a video transcript is available, prefer linking students to that page in
addition to the embedded video.

## Known limits during migration

Some legacy assets still exist mainly as PDFs, older handouts, or embedded
video references. Those materials remain useful, but they may not yet provide
the same level of accessibility support as the newer HTML pages.

When possible, use these pages first:

- [For Students](/docs/students)
- [For Instructors](/curriculum)
- [Videos and Transcripts](/docs/videos)

## If you encounter a barrier

1. Note the page URL or app location.
2. Describe what you were trying to do.
3. Record what happened instead.
4. Include your browser, device type, and assistive technology if relevant.

Use the [Help](/help) page as the reporting entry point until a dedicated
support channel is published on the site.
